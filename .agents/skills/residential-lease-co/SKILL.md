# Residential Lease Contract — Colombian Law (Ley 820 de 2003)

Generate legally sound, complete, and enforceable **Contratos de Arrendamiento de Vivienda Urbana** for Colombian properties.

## Scope

**Use for:** Urban residential lease contracts in Colombia — apartments, houses, rooms (shared housing / pensión), and units under horizontal property (propiedad horizontal).

**Not for:** Commercial leases, rural properties, office spaces, or vacation rentals. Those are governed by the Código Civil and other specific regulations.

---

## Legal Foundation

All contracts must comply with:

- **Ley 820 de 2003** — Primary statute governing urban residential leases in Colombia (enacted July 10, 2003, Diario Oficial 45244). This is the core reference.
- **Código Civil Colombiano** — Title XXVI (Leasing), Books 4, Chapters II and III (supplementary obligations for landlord and tenant).
- **Ley 675 de 2001** — Horizontal property regime (relevant when the property is part of a condominium).
- **Ley 142 de 1994 / Ley 689 de 2001** — Public utility services rules affecting utility deposit caps.
- **Ley 1564 de 2012 (CGP)** — Código General del Proceso; governs eviction proceedings (derogated Articles 35–40 of Ley 820 de 2003 on procedural matters).
- **IPC (Consumer Price Index)** — Published monthly by DANE; used to calculate maximum annual rent increases.

> **Note:** Articles 35–40 of Ley 820 de 2003 (provisional restitution, cautelary measures, single instance) were derogated by Article 626 lit. c) of Ley 1564 de 2012. Eviction proceedings now follow the CGP.

---

## Mandatory Contract Elements (Art. 3, Ley 820)

Every residential lease contract — verbal or written — must include at minimum:

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Parties** | Full legal name and national ID (cédula / NIT) of landlord(s) and tenant(s) |
| **Property identification** | Full address, cadastral or property registry data if available |
| **Partial property** | If only part is leased, describe the portion and shared areas |
| **Rent amount and payment terms** | Monthly amount in COP (or foreign currency with COP payment clause per Art. 19), due date, payment location or account |
| **Ancillary services** | Which utilities/services are included (conexos) or optional (adicionales) |
| **Contract duration** | Agreed term; defaults to 1 year if not stated (Art. 5) |
| **Utility payment responsibility** | Explicitly state whether landlord or tenant pays public utilities |
| **Notification addresses** | Both parties must provide addresses for legal notifications (Art. 12) |

## Contract Classification (Art. 4, Ley 820)

Identify the contract type before drafting — it affects obligations and termination rules:

- **Individual (a):** One or more natural persons renting for personal or family housing, or a legal entity for third-party housing.
- **Mancomunado (b):** Two or more natural persons jointly and severally liable for rent (solidaridad).
- **Compartido (c):** Partial, non-independent unit; shared areas with landlord or other tenants. Must follow government regulations.
- **Pensión (d):** Non-independent unit with mandatory additional services; term under 1 year. Either party may terminate with 10 days' notice, no compensation.

## Rent Rules (Arts. 18–20, Ley 820)

### Maximum Rent Cap (Art. 18)
- Monthly rent **cannot exceed 1% of the property's commercial value**.
- Commercial value for this purpose cannot exceed **2× the current cadastral appraisal (avalúo catastral)**.

### Currency (Art. 19)
- Rent may be agreed in foreign currency but **must be paid in COP** at the market exchange rate (TRM) on the date the obligation was incurred, unless parties agree otherwise.

### Annual Increase (Art. 20)
- After every 12 months under the same price, the landlord **may increase rent by up to 100% of the IPC** (annual CPI) from the immediately preceding calendar year.
- The new rent must still comply with the 1% cap from Art. 18.
- **Procedure:** The landlord must notify the tenant in writing via certified postal service (servicio postal autorizado) or the notification mechanism specified in the contract. Without this notice, the increase is unenforceable. However, voluntary payment of an increase by the tenant waives the right to claim a refund.

## Obligations of the Parties

### Landlord Obligations (Art. 8, Ley 820 + Cód. Civil Cap. II, Tít. XXVI, Lib. 4)

1. Deliver the property in good condition, safe and sanitary, on the agreed date.
2. Maintain the property and its agreed services in good working order for the agreed purpose.
3. If the contract is written, deliver **signed original copies** to the tenant and any co-debtor within **10 days** of signing. Failure triggers a fine of 3 monthly rents (petición de parte required).
4. For horizontal property: deliver a copy of the community regulations (reglamento de propiedad horizontal).
5. For shared housing (vivienda compartida): maintain common areas and internal order.

### Tenant Obligations (Art. 9, Ley 820 + Cód. Civil Cap. III, Tít. XXVI, Lib. 4)

1. Pay rent on time, at the agreed location or account.
2. Take care of the property; repair damage caused by misuse or negligence (not normal wear and tear).
3. Pay utilities and common expenses on time if contractually responsible.
4. Comply with horizontal property regulations and police codes.
5. For shared housing: maintain and repair common areas when damage is their fault.

## Prohibited Clauses — Hard Prohibitions

> [!CAUTION]
> Including any of the following provisions makes them **null and void by law** (Ley 820 de 2003, Art. 16). Do NOT include them in any contract.

- **Cash deposits or real sureties (cauciones reales)** to guarantee tenant obligations — absolutely prohibited. This includes disguised deposits under other names or separate documents.
- Deposits cannot be required indirectly or through third parties.

> **Exception:** Deposits to public utility companies for service guarantees are allowed but capped (Art. 15): no more than 2 consecutive billing periods (cargo fijo + cargo por aportes de conexión + cargo por unidad de consumo), calculated as the average of the last 3 billing periods × 1.5.

---

## Subletting and Assignment (Art. 17, Ley 820)

- The tenant **cannot sublet or assign** the lease without the landlord's **express written authorization**.
- If violated, the landlord may terminate the contract AND/OR enter a new contract with the actual occupants (old contract becomes void). The landlord must communicate this in writing to the tenant.
- Authorized subletting: subtenant may intervene as a joint party (litisconsorte) in judicial proceedings.

---

## Termination Rules

### By Mutual Agreement (Art. 21)
Both parties may terminate at any time by mutual written agreement.

### Landlord-Initiated Termination (Art. 22)

**Due to breach (causales de incumplimiento):**
1. Tenant fails to pay rent within the stipulated period.
2. Tenant fails to pay utilities causing disconnection or service loss, or fails to pay common expenses (expensas comunes).
3. Unauthorized subletting, assignment, or change of use by tenant.
4. Repeated disturbance of neighbors or use for criminal activities (must be proven before police authority).
5. Unauthorized renovations, modifications, or partial/total destruction by tenant.
6. Violation of horizontal property rules.

**During renewals — unilateral termination (Art. 22, num. 7):**
- At any time during a renewal: **3-month written notice** via certified mail + **3 months' rent indemnification**.
- Procedure: notify, then deposit the indemnification with the competent authority within 3 months of notice (Art. 23).

**At lease expiry — special restitution causes (Art. 22, num. 8):**
Written notice ≥ 3 months before expiry, plus one of:
- **(a)** Owner/possessor needs the property for own residence (minimum 1 year).
- **(b)** Demolition for new construction or major repairs requiring vacancy.
- **(c)** Delivery required to fulfill a purchase-sale agreement.
- **(d)** Pure landlord will — but only after the contract has been in force **at least 4 years**; requires 1.5 months' rent indemnization.

For causes (a), (b), (c): landlord must also post a bond (caución) equal to **6 months' rent** in favor of the tenant, guaranteeing fulfillment within 6 months of restitution.

> **Without written pre-notice, the contract automatically renews for the same initial term.**

### Tenant-Initiated Termination (Art. 24)

**Due to breach:**
1. Landlord deliberately suspends utilities or incurs default on payments that cause service loss.
2. Repeated interference by landlord with tenant's quiet enjoyment (proven before police authority).
3. Landlord ignores tenant's legal or contractual rights.

**Unilateral termination during the term or renewals (Art. 24, num. 4):**
- **3-month written notice** via certified mail + **3 months' rent indemnification**.
- Procedure: notify, then deposit indemnification with competent authority (Art. 25).

**At lease expiry (Art. 24, num. 5):**
- **3-month written notice** via certified mail before expiry, **no indemnification required**, no causal needed.
- Without written pre-notice, the contract automatically renews for the same initial term.

### Right of Retention (Art. 26)
If the landlord owes an indemnization to the tenant, the tenant **cannot be forced to vacate** until payment is received or properly secured.

## Rent Payment and Proof (Arts. 10–11, Ley 820)

- Landlord must issue **written proof of payment** (recibo) stating date, amount, and period covered, upon request.
- If landlord refuses to receive rent: tenant must deposit funds via an authorized institution within **5 business days** after the due date and notify landlord by certified mail within 5 days of deposit (Art. 10).
- Failure to follow this consignation procedure puts the tenant in default (mora).

---

## Public Utilities — Liability Rules (Art. 15, Ley 820)

- If tenant is responsible for utilities, landlord must notify the utility company of the lease and submit any guarantees.
- After notification and the end of the next billing period, **landlord's property is released from utility liability**; responsibility falls solely on the tenant.
- If deposits are constituted for utilities, landlord cannot withhold contract compliance for more than **15 business days** after signing if tenant hasn't provided them.

---

## Auto-Renewal (Arts. 6, 22 num. 8, 24 num. 5)

> [!IMPORTANT]
> The contract renews automatically under the exact same terms and for the same initial duration if **neither party** provides timely written notice of termination before the expiry date.

- Notice must be sent via **servicio postal autorizado** (certified mail).
- Minimum notice: **3 months before expiry**.

---

## Notification Address (Art. 12, Ley 820)

- All parties (landlord, tenant, co-debtor, guarantor) must specify a notification address in the contract.
- Address changes must be communicated via certified mail.
- The address on file is the valid legal address for all judicial and extrajudicial notifications.
- If no address is provided: landlord is notified where rent is paid; tenant at the leased property itself.

---

## Solidarity Among Parties (Art. 7, Ley 820)

- Rights and obligations are **joint and several (solidarias)** among multiple landlords and multiple tenants.
- Any landlord may demand performance from any tenant and vice versa.

---

## Landlord Registration (Arts. 28–31, Ley 820)

> [!NOTE]
> This applies when the application or platform manages professional landlords.

- Natural or legal persons whose **primary activity** includes residential leasing (of their own or third-party properties) in municipalities with **more than 15,000 inhabitants** must register (matrícula de arrendador) with the local authority.
- Also required for: owners or sublessors with **more than 5 active contracts**.
- Presumed professional landlord: anyone leasing **more than 10 properties** in the same municipality.
- Registration number must appear in all public advertising.

---

## Rent Discount for Non-Locative Repairs (Art. 27, Ley 820)

- If tenant performs urgent non-locative repairs (repairs the landlord is responsible for under Cód. Civil Art. 1993), the tenant may **deduct the cost from rent**, up to **30% per payment period**.
- If total repair cost exceeds 30%, deductions continue across multiple periods until the balance is recovered.

---

## Inspection, Control, and Sanctions (Arts. 32–34, Ley 820)

- Oversight: **Alcaldía Mayor de Bogotá** (Subsecretaría de Control de Vivienda), **Gobernación de San Andrés**, and **municipal alcaldías**.
- Sanctions: up to **100 monthly minimum wages (SMMLV)** for violations (missing registration, failing to provide contract copies, illegal deposits, etc.).
- Sanctions may also include suspension or cancellation of the landlord's registration.

## Agent Instructions — How to Draft a Contract

### Step 1: Collect Required Data

Before drafting, gather all mandatory fields:

```
- Landlord: Full name, cédula/NIT, address, contact
- Tenant(s): Full name(s), cédula(s), address(es)
- Co-debtor/Guarantor (if any): Full name, cédula, address
- Property: Full address, city, apartment/unit number, cadastral/registry data
- Contract type: Individual / Mancomunado / Compartido / Pensión
- Monthly rent: Amount in COP (or foreign currency with COP clause)
- Payment due date: (e.g., first 5 days of each month)
- Payment method: Bank account, in-person, transfer
- Duration: Start date + end date (or term in months/years)
- Utilities responsibility: Which party pays each utility
- Additional services: Any optional services included/excluded
- Notification addresses: For all parties
- Horizontal property: Yes/No — if yes, include regulations clause
- Co-debtor/fiador: Required or not
```

### Step 2: Validate Legal Constraints

Before generating, check:
- [ ] Rent ≤ 1% of commercial value (≤ 2× avalúo catastral)?
- [ ] No cash deposit or real surety clause?
- [ ] Utility deposit (if any) within legal caps?
- [ ] Subletting explicitly prohibited unless authorized?
- [ ] Contract type correctly identified?
- [ ] Notification addresses included for all parties?
- [ ] Auto-renewal clause included?
- [ ] Termination causes listed for both parties?
- [ ] Annual increase clause referencing IPC?

### Step 3: Structure the Contract

Use this canonical section order:

1. **ENCABEZADO** — Title, city, date
2. **PARTES** — Identification of landlord(s), tenant(s), co-debtor(s)
3. **OBJETO** — Property description and contract classification
4. **CANON DE ARRENDAMIENTO** — Amount, currency, payment terms, due date
5. **SERVICIOS PÚBLICOS Y CONEXOS** — Who pays what
6. **TÉRMINO** — Start/end dates, auto-renewal clause
7. **REAJUSTE DEL CANON** — IPC increase mechanism + notification procedure
8. **OBLIGACIONES DEL ARRENDADOR** — Full list per Art. 8
9. **OBLIGACIONES DEL ARRENDATARIO** — Full list per Art. 9
10. **PROHIBICIONES** — No subletting without written consent, no real deposits
11. **CAUSALES DE TERMINACIÓN** — By landlord (Art. 22) and by tenant (Art. 24)
12. **TERMINACIÓN UNILATERAL CON PREAVISO** — Procedures for Arts. 23 and 25
13. **NOTIFICACIONES** — Addresses for all parties
14. **PROPIEDAD HORIZONTAL** (if applicable) — Regulations compliance clause
15. **SOLIDARIDAD** (if mancomunado) — Joint and several liability clause
16. **DISPOSICIONES FINALES** — Governing law, jurisdiction, dispute resolution
17. **FIRMAS** — Signatures of all parties with date

### Step 4: Language and Format Guidelines

- Write in **formal Spanish** (no colloquialisms).
- Reference specific articles of Ley 820 de 2003 inline (e.g., "de conformidad con el Artículo 20 de la Ley 820 de 2003").
- Use **blank fields with underscores** for user-fillable data: `_______________`.
- Number all clauses sequentially (CLÁUSULA PRIMERA, CLÁUSULA SEGUNDA, etc.).
- Include the full legal date formula: city, day, month, year in words.
- Two original copies must be prepared (one per party), plus additional copies for co-debtors/guarantors if applicable.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

> [!WARNING]
> These are the most frequent errors in Colombian residential leases:

- **Including cash deposit clauses** — null by law, exposes landlord to sanctions.
- **Omitting notification address** — creates legal notification uncertainty.
- **Not specifying who pays utilities** — defaults to tenant, but creates ambiguity.
- **Missing the annual increase notice procedure** — makes increases unenforceable.
- **Skipping the co-debtor identification** — co-debtor clause is unenforceable without full ID.
- **Using a verbal increase notice** — must be certified mail or the agreed contractual method.
- **Not specifying the property portion** for shared housing or pensión contracts.
- **Setting a rent above the 1% commercial value cap** — contract clause is void; court can recalculate.
- **Applying Ley 820 to non-residential leases** — commercial, office, and rural leases are governed by the Código Civil, not Ley 820.
- **Confusing IPC with DTF or prime rates** — only IPC (DANE) is the valid index for residential lease increases.
